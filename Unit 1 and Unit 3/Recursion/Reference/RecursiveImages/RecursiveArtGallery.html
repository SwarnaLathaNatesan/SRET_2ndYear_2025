Explore the fascinating world of recursive art, where patterns repeat themselves in infinite complexity and beauty.

Droste Effect

A picture appears inside itself, creating a recursive loop. Named after Dutch cocoa brand Droste, which used this effect in its packaging.

Mandelbrot Set

The most famous fractal, defined by the simple equation zₙ₊₁ = zₙ² + c. Its boundary reveals progressively finer recursive details at every magnification.

Sierpinski Triangle

A fractal formed by recursively subdividing a triangle into smaller congruent triangles. It's a classic example of self-similarity.

M.C. Escher's Art

Escher's works often featured recursive patterns and impossible structures, playing with perspective and infinity.
