Proving 24 mod 36 = 24

Understanding the Modulus Operation

What is Modulus Operation?

The modulus operation finds the remainder after division of one number by another. For two numbers a and b, "a mod b" is the remainder when a is divided by b.

a mod b = remainder when a is divided by b

Key Property

If a < b, then a mod b = a. This is because if the dividend is smaller than the divisor, the quotient is 0 and the remainder is the dividend itself.

Visualizing 24 mod 36 on a Number Line

Since 24 is less than 36, when we try to divide 24 by 36, it doesn't even complete one full division. The remainder is simply 24.

24 < 36 ⇒ 24 mod 36 = 24

Decimal Proof

Using Decimal Division

We can also prove this using decimal division:

24 ÷ 36 = 0.666...

The integer part is 0, which means 36 goes into 24 zero times.

Remainder = 24 - (36 × 0) = 24

Thus, 24 mod 36 = 24

Division Equation

For any integers a and b, the division algorithm states:

a = b × q + r, where 0 ≤ r < b

In our case, a = 24 and b = 36.

2

Find Quotient and Remainder

We need to find integers q and r such that:

24 = 36 × q + r, with 0 ≤ r < 36

Since 24 < 36, the only solution is q = 0 and r = 24.

3

Apply Modulus Definition

By definition, the modulus is the remainder r:

24 mod 36 = r = 24
